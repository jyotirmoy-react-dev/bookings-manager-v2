TEST CODE SHEET-WRAS CLARIFICATION

In the UK, as you might be aware, water undertakers are responsible for enforcement of the Water Supply (Water Fittings) Regulations, Byelaws in Scotland. As part of their enforcement activity, water suppliers have raised concerns with the Scheme about the identification of markings on WRAS approved products. To ensure that all WRAS approved products are readily identifiable when installed the WRAS Product Assessment Group (PAG) has been considering the requirements of Regulators’ Specification Test Code Sheet (TCS) 6001.1 ‘identification’.

Recognising the need to provide clear guidance that sets out the Scheme’s marking requirements the PAG has developed a clarification document. Furthermore having completed a consultation process the Scheme has agreed that with effect from 1st July 2015 it will be a mandatory requirement that all new applications seeking approval for products which require marking will have to demonstrate compliance with the marking requirements as defined in the attached clarification document.

The following information corresponds to the mentioned clarification issued recently by WRAS to the test code sheet 6001.1. This information should be read in conjunction with the below Test Code Sheet 6001.1 (pages 2 to 4 of the current document). It is intended to aid the test laboratories and manufacturers in clarifying the test and acceptance criteria requirements.

NOTE: This clarification applies to TCS 6001.1 Issue 7 Clause (A)

_________________________________________________________________________________________


ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

All marking must be readily visible and distinguishable after installation and on an external surface. For concealed fittings, the marking must be visible when exposed for maintenance, repair or inspection.

Note: Where approval is sought for a component which is to be installed within a larger fitting/assembly, e.g. check valve cartridge or tap headwork, the marking must still be present but it does not need to be visible after assembly and installation.

The identification mark must not be easily removed. Accepted methods are etched, cast, printed, moulded, embossed or data plate. These are examples of more commonly used marking types, but the list provided should not be regarded as exhaustive.

The mark must include reference to the manufacturers or factors name or company logo.

In addition the WRAS approval holder may choose to add, but is not compulsory, the range or model name and the WRAS Approved product logo or WRAS approved.
Date of Implementation: July 1st 2015
Applications submitted to the test laboratory on or after this date must meet requirements highlighted above when read in conjunction with TCS 6001.1

(A) ALL FITTINGS

TEST METHOD

Every fitting and appliance shall be examined to establish that it is marked, labelled or plated in an appropriate manner
to enable its course, purpose, application and manner of installation to be identified.

5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

The mark, label or plate shall include reference to the manufacturer or factor and any other information necessary to enable it to be clearly identified. Certain fittings and appliances shall include other information in the marking or on
the label or plate (see overleaf),

The identification mark shall be made on, or the label or plate attached to, the fitting or appliance where it will be accessible after installation so far as is reasonably practicable.

Marking shall be of an appropriate permanent nature by means of acid etching, embossing, rolling-in, stamping, a soldered-on plate etc.

(D) TUBES AND PIPE

5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

Tube and pipe of every material to an applicable standard deemed to satisfy byelaw requirements shall be marked in accordance with the requirements of that standard. Copper tube to BS 2871 Part 1 additionally shall be marked with
the month and year of manufacture adjacent to each marking required by that standard.

Tube and pipe of every material otherwise shall be marked permanently by a means (not labelled) at intervals not exceeding 500mm with the type and/or class of material, the manufacturers means of identification and the year of manufacture.

5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

Backflow protection devices shall be marked permanently and visibly on the casing, or on a fixed data plate.

(e) Acoustic group.

(g) Reference (type or model...)

(F) WC SUITES
(Derived from The Regulators Spec. for WC Suites: March 2000)

5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
Every flushing cistern or trough for use with a water closet pan shall be marked with the following;

(i) The pan and cistern shall have a manufacturers identification / model number permanently
marked in such a position that it will be readily visible once the suite is installed.

(ii) The inside of the cistern shall be indelibly marked with:

(a) a permanent mark indicating the water level at which the float operated valve is to shut off to give the
appropriate volume of water for flushing purposes.
(b) an indication of the flush volume.

For flushing cisterns designed to give flushes of different volumes, there shall be instructions clearly and permanently marked on the cistern or displayed nearby, for operating it to obtain the different volumes of flush.
